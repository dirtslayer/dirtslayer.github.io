FRP is factory reset protection and its not new. 

But, if you haven't had to wrestle with it you may not be aware how it impacts you. 

FRP is intended to prevent thieves from wiping a device and reselling it. If you are unaware of it, you can brick your device.

In the past I found it convenient to create a new email for devices that are 'floaters.' They are used around the house by anyone (but not for email or anything important). I would create an email account per device. Recently my huge mistake was that I never bothered to save the email because I did not know about FRP. 

FRP doesn't turn itself on, FRP is enabled when you put a screen lock on. My daughter enabled FRP in this way. Then she started a factory reset, was able to wipe the device, but we could not login to the set up wizard without logging into the forgotten account. 

I wasted nearly a whole day. I tried many things including using odin and adb. This whole episode is embarrassing. It is frustrating to me because it is preventable.

FRP is bad for consumers. It is bad for the second hand device market.

Happy googling, NOT!
