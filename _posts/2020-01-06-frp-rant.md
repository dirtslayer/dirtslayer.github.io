
FRP is factory reset protection and its not new, but if you haven't had to wrestle with it you may not be aware how it impacts you. FRP is intended to prevent thieves from wiping a phone and reselling it. It works well, so, if you are unaware of it, you can brick your device if your not careful. In the past I found it convenient to create a new email for devices that are 'floaters' which get used around the house (not for email or anything important). My huge mistake was that I never bothered to write down or use the email because I did not know about FRP. FRP is enabled when you put a screen lock on. My daughter enabled FRP in this way. Then she started a factory reset, was able to wipe the device, but we could not login to the set up wizard without logging into the forgotten account. 

What is frustrating to me is how preventable the situation is: it cost me a device and I hope to never encounter FRP again.
